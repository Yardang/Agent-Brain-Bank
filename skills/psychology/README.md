## Jungian Psychology
Jungian psychology, also known as analytical psychology, focuses on the deep aspects of the human psyche. It offers insights into the conscious and unconscious minds through concepts like archetypes, the collective unconscious, and individuation. Here are some key aspects:

### Key Concepts
- **Archetypes**: Universal symbols and themes that arise in dreams, myths, and fairy tales.
- **Collective Unconscious**: A part of the unconscious mind shared among beings of the same species, containing memories and archetypes.
- **Individuation**: The process of integrating different parts of the self to achieve personal development.

### Applications
- Personal development and self-awareness
- Understanding interpersonal relationships
- Therapeutic practices and guiding individuals through personal crises.

### Techniques
- Dream Analysis: Understanding symbols and messages in dreams.
- Active Imagination: A technique to explore unconscious content by engaging with it creatively.

Jungian psychology provides valuable tools for personal growth and understanding the complexities of the human experience.
